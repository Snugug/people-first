empathy (n.)
Modeled on the German Einfühlung; from ein "in" + Fühlung "feeling". Coined in 1858 by the German philosopher Rudolf Lotze as a translation of the Greek empatheia or "passion, state of emotion"; an assimilated form of en "in" and pathos "feeling". From a theory of art appreciation that maintains appreciation depends on the viewer's ability to project his personality into the viewed object.
